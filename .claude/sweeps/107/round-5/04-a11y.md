## CONTRAST-SCRIPT CORRECTION (affects severity of findings below)

Round-2/round-3 used `.claude/sweeps/107/round-2/contrast.mjs`, whose `*-on-white*` rows are BUGGED: passing the white card as the RGB array `[255,255,255]` into `contrast()` mis-resolves, producing garbage (e.g. it reported `overdue-text #b45b00 on white = 1.82` and `bad #e7000b on white = 1.80`). Re-derived with a corrected calculator (`.claude/sweeps/107/round-5/amber-bad-surfaces.mjs`, hex-only, verified against first principles):

| fg | on canvas `#f8fafc` | on white card `#fff` | on its own @18% tint |
|---|---|---|---|
| overdue base `#e17100` (light) | 3.06 ✗ | 3.20 ✗ | 2.75 ✗ |
| overdue-text `#b45b00` (light) | **4.52 ✓** | **4.73 ✓** | 4.06 ✗ |
| bad base/text `#e7000b` (light) | **4.56 ✓** | **4.77 ✓** | 3.30 ✗ |

Consequences:
1. `--status-bad` as TEXT passes AA on BOTH light surfaces (canvas 4.56 / white card 4.77) and dark (5.29). Round-3's claim that bad-on-white-cards fails (1.80) was the script bug — **`status-bad`-as-text is NOT a finding on any solid surface** (the round-4 base→`-bad-text` migrations were harmless no-ops since light `-bad-text == base`).
2. `--status-overdue-text` (the token round-4 created) reaches AA on BOTH light solid surfaces (4.52 / 4.73). So **amber base used as text on a SOLID surface (canvas or white card) IS a real failure, and migrating it to `-text` fixes it** — exactly the fix round-4 applied to badge/expiry/profile/date-group but did NOT propagate to the warning/alert sites below.
3. Both status colors on their OWN @18% tint stay sub-4.5 even with `-text` — this is the DEF-R4-A11Y-3 ceiling (registered for amber badge/expiry only).
